The 0x Engineer

You always hear about the 10x, 1x, even 100x engineers. But what about the 0x engineer?

A 0x Engineer...

  • Let's you know up front that they are using hexadecimal
  • Had a favorite set of memory addresses before ASLR ruined their fun
  • Consumes moderate amounts of 0xC0FFEE to meet realistic deadlines
  • Remembers most of the manufacturer ID numbers in Mac addresses by heart (00041F is their favorite)
  • Knows the optimal way to communicate back with NASA should they get stranded on Mars with the Pathfinder probe
  • Prefers to call March 14th "3.243F6 Day"
  • Constantly confuses Halloween and Christmas
  • Tries to make the joke "there are 0x02 types of people in this world; those who understand Hexadecimal and those who don't" then gets confused when people just stare at them and not laugh
  • Uses their favorite numbers as colors for their web site
  • Is the only person in the world who passes something other than "10" to JavaScript's parseInt() radix parameter
